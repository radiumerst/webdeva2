Japan's Must Knows

Important things for you to know about Japan.

Street Food

Street Food can be quite common in Japan, however there are some must knows about the culture.

Japanese people tend to stay near the shop or stall that they bought the food from, and refrain from eating and drinking while walking.

Furthermore, it could be difficult to find public bins in Japan, only in convenience stores and at the hotel.

Phrases

These are some good-to-know phrases for you to say while in Japan!

Arigatou gozaimasu: Thank you

Gochisosama deshita: Thank you for the meal

Sumimasen: Excuse me/Sorry

Kore o onegaishimasu: Can I have this?

Akihabara

Akihabara, located just 2 stops from Tokyo Station, is known as the anime & electronics hub of Tokyo. It is a bustling city with an entire street full of anime merch and electronics, if you are an anime fan, or a game-geek, this is the place for you!

Asakusa

Asakusa is the home to the world famous shrine, where people pray and wish. No matter if you are interested in Japanese culture or not, Asakusa is a must-visit as it is a sight to behold.

Shibuya

Shibuya, a bustling city with numerous shops around. Trendy fashion goods, department shops, shopping complexes, all in one city. It is also the place with the famous Hachiko statue, the famous loyal dog who waited at Shibuya station for its owner to return.

Shibuya Sky

If you're at Shibuya, you definitely need to visit Shibuya Sky, especially during sunset! It gives you a huge birdseye view of the entire Tokyo landscape, all while enjoying the outdoor winds 47 stories up. During the sunset, it gives out a bright orange hue all around the Tokyo landscape.

JAL Museum

Aviation enthusiast? JAL Museum is for you! JAL Museum is an aircraft museum constructed by Japan Airlines, bringing you to understand more about the history and current legacy of airplanes and airlines! The guided tour even brings you down to both of their hangars, where you get to see planes on the outside closer than ever before!

Teamlab Planets

Whether you are interested in art or not doesn't matter. Teamlab Planets brings you through an all-interactive art museum, even into knee-deep water! It is simply a calming and relaxing experience, accustomed to all.

The Railway Museum

This is a little far, but it is worth it. Located in Omiya, nothern Tokyo, the Railway Museum has all types of trains for you to see and discover. Bringing you back in time with the olden trains, to the current Shinkansen models! The building itself is located next to train tracks, so you can see actual trains zoom right past!

Cup Noodles Museum Yokohama

Cup Noodles Museum is definitely a place to visit for a fun family activity, or even with friends! You get to draw on your own cup noodles cup, and even choose exactly what ingredients you want in your cup noodles!

Minatomirai

Minatomirai is where the Cup Noodles Museum is located. However, it is also a place for you to explore, a calming place that feels both modern, yet slow-paced like the countryside. It boasts a beautiful view into the Tokyo bay, and even have a small amusement park in the centre for you to have fun!

Shinsaibashi

A popular shopping district for both locals and tourists alike. Also home to the famous Glico Running Man! Even if you are not here to shop, it is nice to head to the bridge and wind down as you hear some local buskers perform!

Osaka Castle

The old medieval castle of Osaka. Brings you back in time of the olden construction. You may not need to enter the museum, but the surroundings and its nature is calming and good for relaxation, all while seeing this piece of the past.

Universal Studios Japan

Even if you've been to Universal Studios in other countries before, you definitely need to visit Universal Studios Japan. It is nothing like anywhere else. They oftentimes host collaborations with popular animes and games, such as Detective Conan, and Mario! This is a photo of the inside of Super Nintendo World, and it was a blast exploring the inside!

Fushimi Inari

Fushimi Inari is a very famous shrine, and that is not for no reason! It is a really beautiful stretch of shrines and park, a really calming experience overall and an amazing photo spot, even outside the world famous thousands of gates!

Gion

Gion is famous for its Geisha district, though unfortunately it has been closed off to the public due to rowdy foreigners harrassing geishas. Though the main public street is still open, you might not be have such a great experience.

*Note: This is not my photo, as photography was highly prohibited in the area of Gion I was in.

Miyajima

Miyajima Island is a really pretty island full of deers just off the coast from Hiroshima. There is a world famous gate on the shores of the island, whic its floor eventually gets covered by the ocean as the tides rolls in. It is really beautiful and calming, and has a really good matcha house. Highly recommended!

War Memorial Peace Museum

World War II was bad, really bad. However, despite being at the centre of the bombing, Hiroshima still has a War Memorial Museum, describing about the pains of its past, and wishing for it to not happen to anybody else. Head over to the museum and learn about its past, and of course be respectful around.

Visit Japan Web

Visit Japan Web is the Japanese pre-immigration page. You will need to pre-register your immigration and customs declaration on the website for a smoother process into Japan.

Transportation IC Card

Currently, at least in Tokyo, transportation IC cards are really hard to come by. Suica can only be purchased in the main ticket office at Tokyo Station.

If you have an Apple device, lucky for you! You can install a virtual Suica card into your Apple wallet and top-up using your credit/debit card!

Money

Despite being a modern society, many shops in Japan, especially small local ones accept cash only.

Furthermore, if you plan to get a physical Transportation IC Card, you can only recharge using cash.
